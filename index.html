CAR-D Features

What is CAR-D?

CAR-D is a web-based portal for collection & compilation, analysis and dissemination of all the cargo and cruise movement data for IWAI and its stakeholders. Portal gives access of the data to general public showcasing IWAI capabilities and potential of inland waterways transport.

Integrated portal for data on NWs

Provides cargo and passenger data for all National Waterways in a single integrated portal Assimilates data across different jetties (river and coastal), shippers and operators Workflow approval based data entry ensures accuracy of data and removes manual dependency of reporting

Transparency Data

Provides access to all stakeholders and general public thus bringing trust in the data being reported for IWT sector Assists potential shippers & cargo owners in identifying cargo type and commodity at various waterways, terminals and jetties. Has near real time capturing of cargo and passenger traffic with the help of terminal operators and regional offices of IWAI

Analytical Dashboards

Provides the key traffic trends and patterns easily enabling stakeholders to take better understand the current and future protection of IWT Provides analysis w.r.t National Waterways, Commodities, Shippers, Jetties which is available y-o-y and m-o-m with detailed reports

Inland Waterways Authority of India (IWAI)

IWAI is a statutory body of the Ministry of Shipping, Government of India (GoI). IWAI was set up in 1986 for regulation and development of Inland Waterways for the purposes of shipping and navigation. IWAI is primarily responsible for development, maintenance and regulation of Inland Water Transport (IWT) on the National Waterways (NWs) in the country. Presently, there are 111 new NWs in the country.

Portal for Assets And Navigational Information(PANI)

PANI is a digital service by IWAI that provides key systematic and aggregated River and Navigation information related to National Waterways in India for facilitating transportation of cargo and other vessels through National Waterways. This will help in better understanding of the key features of the NW that are essential for decision making on use of waterways mode for transportation.
